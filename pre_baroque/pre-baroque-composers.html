Pre-Baroque Composers

Medieval Time Period (0-1400)

  • Most music at this time did not have notation and was played by ear, so there were not many composers
  • Stephen of Liège - wrote the Gregorian Chant

    • Renaissance Time Period (1400-1600)

  • Orlando de Lassus - Master of polyphony>, energetic rhythms, and text painting
  • Giovanni Pierluigi da Palestrina - helped refine polyphony and chromaticism (the use of notes foreign to the key with diatonic pitches - accidentals)
  • Thomas Tallis - lived during the time between the intense conflict between Catholicism and Protestantism (had to adjust his music style to his monarch's beliefs)
  • William Byrd - pupil of Thomas Tallis, developed keyboard music
